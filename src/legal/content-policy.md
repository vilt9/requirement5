# Content Policy

**Last updated: 17 July 2026**

This Content Policy forms part of our [Terms of Service](/terms). It explains what you may and may not put on Requirement5, how moderation works, and how to report a problem. It applies to everything you upload or type that others can see: card images, card names and descriptions, set names, tags, and your username.

---

## 1. What is not allowed

Do not upload, publish, or share content that:

- **is unlawful** in the UK, or helps anyone break the law;
- **sexualises minors in any way** — child sexual abuse material (CSAM) is never tolerated, is reported to the relevant authorities, and results in an immediate permanent ban;
- **is pornographic, obscene, or gratuitously violent**;
- **is hateful, harassing, threatening, or defamatory**, including slurs and content that attacks people based on race, ethnicity, religion, sex, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or similar;
- **infringes intellectual-property rights** — using images, characters, logos, or artwork you do not have the right to use;
- **contains someone's personal data or likeness without their consent**;
- **impersonates** a person or organisation;
- **contains malware**, or links to it; or
- is **spam**, or attempts to manipulate the in-game economy.

Usernames, card names, set names, and tags are also screened for slurs and profanity when you create them.

---

## 2. You are responsible for what you upload

By publishing a card you confirm that you own it or have the rights to use it, that it does not infringe anyone's rights, and that it does not breach this policy. See clause 6 of the [Terms](/terms).

---

## 3. How moderation works

**Reporting.** Anyone can report a card — logged in or not — using the report control on the card. Choose a reason (for example nudity/sexual content, violence, copyright, hate, or other) and, if you like, add detail.

**Taken out of circulation immediately.** When a card is reported it is **removed from circulation straight away** — it disappears from the pool, from draws, from tag and set pages, and can no longer be saved — while it waits for review. This is deliberate: a potentially harmful image stops being shown the moment someone flags it.

**Review.** A human reviews reported cards and then either **restores** the card (if the report was mistaken) or **removes** it for good.

**Automated checks.** We operate, or are adding, automated screening of uploaded images and text to catch unsafe or illegal content, alongside the reporting flow above.

---

## 4. What happens if you break this policy

Depending on severity, we may: remove the content; reverse related /t26 transactions; restrict features on your account; or **suspend or permanently ban** your account. Illegal content — CSAM above all — is reported to the appropriate authorities.

---

## 5. Appeals

If you believe your card was removed by mistake, email **hi@requirement5.com** with the card link and we will take another look.
