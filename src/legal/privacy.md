# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 17 July 2026**

---

## Summary (the short version)

Requirement5 is a web app and command-line tool for making, collecting, and trading holographic digital trading cards in a play-money economy. This is the plain-English version of what we do with your data. The numbered sections below are the full detail.

- **Who runs it:** Odd Solutions Ltd (England & Wales, company number 16186575). Contact us at **hi@requirement5.com**.
- **What we collect:** the details you give us to have an account (email, username, a hashed password, your date of birth), the images and cards you upload, records of any purchases you make, plus some technical data (server logs, IP address) and first-party analytics.
- **What's public:** the cards you publish, your username, and your collections are **visible to everyone**. Your email, password, and date of birth are never public.
- **Money:** the play-money currency (/t26) is earned by playing. You can optionally buy it with real money. Payments run through **Stripe** — we never see or store your card number.
- **We don't sell your data** and we don't use third-party advertising or tracking networks.
- **You must be 18 or over** to use Requirement5.
- **Your rights:** you can ask to see, correct, export, or delete your data. Email **hi@requirement5.com**.
- **Complaints:** you can complain to the UK's Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

## 2. What personal data we collect

**2.1 Account data.** When you register we collect your **email address**, a **username** you choose, a **password** (stored only in hashed form), and your **date of birth** (used to confirm you are 18 or over).

**2.2 Uploaded images (card art).** You can upload custom images to use as card art. These may themselves contain personal data. You are responsible for having the right to upload what you upload (section 6).

**2.3 Card content and profile data that is public.** The cards you publish (name, description, tags, the set they belong to), your **username**, and your **collections** are published to a public pool and are visible to other users and, potentially, to anyone on the internet.

**2.4 Purchase records (payments).** If you buy virtual currency with real money, we store **minimal purchase records** (that a purchase happened, the amount, currency, a Stripe reference, and the date). **We do not receive or store your full card number, CVC, or bank details** — Stripe handles those (section 7).

**2.5 Virtual economy data.** We hold records of your **/t26 balance**, cards you own, and your gameplay activity, so the play-money economy works.

**2.6 Server logs and IP address.** Our servers automatically record technical information — including your **IP address**, request times, endpoints accessed, browser/agent information, and error diagnostics.

**2.7 Analytics.** We use **first-party cohort analytics** to understand how the Service is used in aggregate. We do **not** use third-party advertising or cross-site tracking networks.

**2.8 Local-storage authentication token.** When you log in, we store a token in your browser's local storage so you stay logged in.

We do **not** intentionally collect special category data. Please do not upload it (section 6).

## 4. The play-money economy and payments

4.1 The in-app currency **/t26** is play-money, primarily **earned by playing**. You may **optionally buy** /t26 with real money.

4.2 Real-money payments are processed by **Stripe**. When you pay, your card details go directly to Stripe. We keep only minimal purchase records (section 2.4) so we can credit your account, provide support, handle refunds and chargebacks, and meet our tax/accounting duties.

## 5. What's public — and what that means

5.1 The following are **public by design**: cards you publish (name, description, tags, set), the **art** you attach to public cards, your **username**, and your **collections**. They may be visible to anyone on the internet, indexed by search engines, or cached by third parties outside our control.

5.2 **Your email address, password, and date of birth are never public.**

5.3 Think carefully before publishing. Don't put private information into a username, card name, description, or tag, and don't upload art containing other people's personal data without their agreement (section 6).

5.4 Because published content lives in a shared, public economy, deleting your account does not automatically un-publish everything you ever contributed — see section 11.

## 6. Your responsibilities for what you upload

6.1 You must only upload images and content that you have the right to use and publish.

6.2 Do **not** upload images containing **other people's personal data** unless you have their permission, and do **not** upload special category data.

6.3 We may remove content that breaches these rules or the law. A reported card is taken out of circulation immediately, pending review (see our Content Policy).

## 11. Deleting your account — and what happens to your public cards

11.1 You can ask us to delete your account at any time by emailing **hi@requirement5.com**.

11.2 When you delete your account, we delete or anonymise your **account data** and remove your private data from active systems.

11.3 **Public content:** because cards, trades, and collections exist in a shared, public economy, some content you published may **persist after deletion** — cards you created may have been collected or traded by other users. Where we can, we will **disassociate** that content from your identity (for example by anonymising your username), but we may not be able to withdraw every published item from circulation.

11.4 We will always keep the minimum we are **legally required** to keep (for example purchase records for tax).

11.5 If you want specific published items taken down, tell us and we will do what we reasonably and lawfully can.

## 12. Cookies and local storage

12.1 The main item we store is an **authentication token in your browser's local storage**, which keeps you logged in. This is **strictly necessary** for the Service to function, so it does not require consent.

12.2 We use **first-party cohort analytics** only. We do **not** use third-party advertising cookies or cross-site tracking.

12.3 You can clear browser storage at any time via your browser settings, but doing so will log you out.
